Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Nexora may process information when you use the VPN app, visit the website, contact support, manage a subscription, or interact with security and diagnostic features. It is intended to give users a practical, transparent view of data handling across the service.

Product: Nexora VPN Focus: transparency, minimization, supportability

1. Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy covers information connected with the Nexora mobile app, website, support communications, subscription handling, product reliability, analytics, and security operations. It explains the main categories of data that may be involved, the reasons for processing, the situations where sharing may occur, and the choices users may have.

Because Nexora is a privacy-focused VPN product, clarity matters. Users should understand the difference between information needed to run a connection, information needed to answer a support request, and purchase information handled through app stores or payment providers.

2. Information You Choose to Provide

You may choose to send us information directly. For example, you might provide a name, email address, message details, purchase context, or troubleshooting notes when you contact support, ask about the product, request subscription help, or report a technical problem. If Nexora adds account recovery, waitlist, or similar support features, you may also provide the identifiers needed to complete those requests.

We use user-provided information to reply, investigate issues, keep reasonable support records, improve response quality, and follow up on requests you started. Please avoid sending sensitive information that is not needed for the request.

3. App, Device, and Operational Data

Like most mobile products, Nexora may process limited technical information needed to deliver and maintain the service. Depending on the feature and platform, this can include app version, device model, operating system version, language setting, approximate region, crash reports, performance measurements, subscription entitlement signals, feature status, or abuse-prevention indicators.

For a VPN service, some connection-related metadata may be processed long enough to establish sessions, route traffic, protect infrastructure, monitor server health, manage capacity, detect outages, and review service incidents. Nexora is designed around collecting only what is reasonably needed for those operational purposes.

This policy describes processing categories at a practical level. Any more specific no-logs, limited-logs, or minimization statements shown in the app or marketing materials should match the service's actual operations and this policy.

4. Information Related to Purchases and Billing

If you purchase a plan, begin a trial, restore access, or ask for billing support, Nexora may receive purchase status, plan level, renewal state, order identifiers, storefront or country information, and similar entitlement signals from the app store or payment platform that manages the transaction.

Billing-related information is generally used to unlock paid features, confirm subscription status, answer support questions, address fraud or abuse, and keep necessary business records. Sensitive payment card details are typically handled by the payment provider or app store rather than by this website.

5. How We Use Information

Nexora may use information for the following business and service purposes:

  • Providing and maintaining VPN connectivity and related app features.
  • Authenticating purchases, restoring entitlements, and managing subscription access.
  • Monitoring service reliability, crash behavior, latency, outages, and infrastructure health.
  • Preventing abuse, spam, fraud, automated misuse, or attacks against the service.
  • Improving onboarding, usability, support quality, and product performance over time.
  • Responding to support inquiries, refund requests, or account-related questions that you submit.
  • Meeting legal, regulatory, tax, security, or compliance obligations where required.

6. What We Do Not Intend This Policy to Permit

Nexora is positioned as a privacy-oriented service, so this policy is not meant to authorize unrelated advertising profiles, hidden resale of browsing behavior, or broad secondary use of user information. This page should not be read as permission for unlimited collection.

The intended model is restrained: use the information reasonably required to run the app, protect the network, manage subscriptions, improve reliability, and assist users who contact us.

7. Sharing of Information

We may share information with service providers that help operate Nexora, but only where it is reasonably necessary for their role. Depending on the product stack, this may include hosting providers, analytics tools, customer support systems, email services, crash-reporting vendors, subscription processors, infrastructure security providers, and app store operators.

We may also share information:

  • When required by law, regulation, subpoena, court order, or other valid legal process.
  • When we believe disclosure is reasonably necessary to protect users, investigate abuse, or defend the service.
  • In connection with a merger, financing, asset sale, acquisition, or other business restructuring, subject to applicable confidentiality and data-handling expectations.

8. Data Minimization and Retention

We aim to keep information only for as long as it is reasonably needed for the relevant purpose. Retention periods may differ for service operations, crash diagnostics, subscription handling, support tickets, legal requests, fraud review, or accounting records.

Some records may be removed quickly, while others may need to be kept longer to enforce agreements, resolve disputes, detect misuse, satisfy accounting requirements, or comply with law. When data is no longer needed, we aim to delete, anonymize, or de-identify it where appropriate.

9. Permissions and Device Controls

Nexora may request device permissions tied to specific features, such as notifications for service updates or subscription-related reminders. Permission prompts should have a clear product purpose, and users can generally manage them through device settings.

Denying a permission may affect how a feature works, but users should not be forced into granting permissions that are unrelated to the core function being requested.

10. Security Measures

We use administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to reduce the risk of unauthorized access, misuse, accidental disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These safeguards may include access controls, role separation, infrastructure monitoring, credential management, service hardening, and restricted support workflows.

No internet or mobile system can be guaranteed to be perfectly secure. Users should also protect their own devices, app store accounts, passwords, and recovery channels, because product security works best when provider controls and user hygiene reinforce each other.

11. International Use

Nexora may be used from different countries and may rely on vendors or infrastructure that operate across jurisdictions. As a result, information may be processed in locations where privacy rules differ from the place where you live. Where cross-border processing occurs, we aim to use safeguards that are appropriate for the service and the legal frameworks that apply.

12. Your Choices and Requests

Depending on where you live and how the service is structured, you may have choices related to permissions, marketing communications, support submissions, diagnostics participation, or deletion and access requests. We encourage users to review device controls, app store settings, and any in-app privacy controls that may be offered.

  • You can stop using the app at any time by uninstalling it.
  • You can manage permissions such as notifications through your device settings.
  • You can contact support if you want help with privacy, billing, or data-related questions.

13. Children’s Privacy

Nexora is not directed to children and is not intended for use by children below the age threshold that applies under relevant law without appropriate authorization. If you believe a child has submitted personal information through the website or support flows, contact us so the situation can be reviewed.

14. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect product evolution, legal obligations, vendor changes, operational practices, or clarifications to how the service works. When we update the policy, we will post the revised version on this page and revise the effective date.

15. Contact

Questions about privacy, data handling, or this policy can be sent through the contact form on the main site: Get in Touch.
